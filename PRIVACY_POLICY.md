# Privacy Policy for Chromap

**Last updated:** December 2024

## Introduction

Chromap ("we", "our", or "the extension") is a Chrome extension that extracts and displays colors from web pages. This privacy policy explains how we handle your data.

## Data Collection

**Chromap does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data.**

The extension operates entirely locally on your device:

- Color extraction happens in your browser on the current webpage
- No data is sent to external servers
- No data is stored on external servers
- No analytics or tracking is performed
- No user behavior is monitored

## How Chromap Works

1. When you click the extension icon, it analyzes the current webpage
2. Colors are extracted from the page's DOM and CSS
3. Colors are displayed in the extension popup
4. All processing happens locally in your browser
5. No data leaves your device

## Permissions

Chromap requires the following permissions:

- **activeTab**: To access the current tab's content for color extraction
- **scripting**: To inject content scripts that analyze the webpage

## Third-Party Services

Chromap does not use any third-party services, analytics, or tracking tools.

## Data Storage

- All data processing happens in memory
- No persistent storage is used
- Color data is not saved or cached

## Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected in this document with an updated "Last updated" date.

## Contact

If you have any questions about this privacy policy, please contact us through the extension's repository or issue tracker.

## Compliance

This extension complies with:

- Chrome Web Store Privacy Requirements
- General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
- California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)

Since no data is collected, there are no data subject rights to exercise - your data never leaves your device.
